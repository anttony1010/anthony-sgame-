01 Game Overview

UNO Dice is a twist on the classic UNO card game where special cards trigger dice rolls that determine how powerful their effects are. Skip and Plus cards no longer have fixed values — roll the die to find out your fate.

Blank cards introduce 6 wild effects tied to dice faces. Rolling a 1 could end your game entirely, while rolling a 6 lets you steal everyone's special cards. If any player ever holds 25 or more cards, they are immediately eliminated.

02 Game Goal

The goal is to be the first player to empty their hand without ever accumulating 25 or more cards. Luck, strategy, and your dice rolls determine survival.

✓ Win Condition
Empty your hand first
✗ Lose Condition
Reach 25+ cards OR roll a 1 on a blank card
⚂

03 Player Controls / Turn Actions

  • Place a card Play a matching color or number — next player's turn begins
  • Skip or +2 / +4 Roll the die — the number shown is how many players are skipped or how many cards are drawn
  • Blank card Roll the die — resolve the effect matching the face shown (1–6 effects, see Mechanics)
  • Draw If you can't play, draw one card and pass your turn
  • Call UNO! When you have exactly 1 card left — call it before anyone else does or draw 2

04 Game Mechanics & Logic

The Blank Card — 6 Dice Effects:

🎲 Roll Effect
⚀Roll 1You are OUT of the game (eliminated)
⚁Roll 2Every player draws 10 cards
⚂Roll 3Put down all your number cards
⚃Roll 4Draw cards until you have UNO (1 card)
⚄Roll 5Steal everyone's special cards
⚅Roll 6YOU draw 10 cards

Variables:

eliminatedAt25 cardsPlayer is out when hand ≥ 25
diceValue1–6Result of each roll — determines effect or amount
skipCountvariesHow many turns are skipped after a Skip/Plus roll

If / Then Rules:

  • IF a card is placed → THEN turn passes to next player
  • IF Skip/Plus is played → THEN roll dice; use result as skip/draw amount
  • IF Blank card is played → THEN roll dice; trigger effect 1–6
  • IF player rolls 1 on blank → THEN that player is eliminated
  • IF player's hand reaches 25+ → THEN player is eliminated
  • IF player has 1 card left → THEN player MUST call "UNO" immediately
  • IF player's hand reaches 0 → THEN that player wins
⚄

05 Game Loop

These steps repeat every turn until only one player remains or someone wins:

  1. Active player checks if they have a playable card
  2. If yes: play the card. If no: draw 1 and pass
  3. If a Skip/Plus card was played: roll dice → apply that number
  4. If a Blank card was played: roll dice → resolve that effect
  5. Check each player's hand count — eliminate anyone at 25+
  6. Check if any player has 0 cards → declare winner
  7. If no winner yet, advance turn and repeat
⚅

06 Playtesting & Changes

Changes made after testing with real players:

⚠ Problem Found

Games ended too quickly because players kept rolling eliminations on blank cards.

✓ Changes Made

Added a "re-roll once" rule — if you roll a 1 on the blank card, you may re-roll one time. If you roll 1 again, you're eliminated.

⚠ Problem Found

The "steal everyone's special cards" effect (roll 5) felt too powerful and unbalanced the game.

✓ Changes Made

Limited the steal to only adjacent players (left and right), not all players at the table.

08 Designer Reflection

The hardest part of designing UNO Dice was coming up with exactly 6 balanced effects for the blank card — one per dice face. The challenge was making each effect feel different and exciting without any single one being completely game-breaking or useless.

My game uses luck as its core tension: you never know what the dice will give you. A Skip card could skip one person or six. A Blank card could save you or end your game on the spot. That unpredictability is what makes it exciting and different from regular UNO.

If I could change one thing, I would add a "mercy rule" for the elimination mechanic so new players don't get knocked out too early and lose interest in the game.
